# Pet Product Warehouse Receiving Standard Operating Procedure

**Document ID:** PET-SOP-010
**Effective Date:** March 1, 2024
**Owner:** Director, Pet Warehouse Operations
**Applies To:** All receiving docks at PetStore distribution centers

---

## 1. Purpose

This SOP defines the standard process for receiving inbound pet product shipments at PetStore warehouses to ensure accurate inventory, product safety compliance, and timely processing for retail partner fulfillment.

## 2. Pre-Arrival

1. Verify purchase order status in the procurement system
2. Confirm expected delivery date and carrier information
3. Allocate receiving dock and staging area (temperature-controlled for food/health products)
4. Prepare receiving documentation (PO copy, inspection checklist, safety cert requirements)
5. For pet food shipments: verify cold-chain staging area availability

## 3. Arrival and Unloading

1. Verify carrier and shipment documentation against PO
2. Inspect packaging for visible damage before unloading
3. For temperature-sensitive products: verify cold-chain temperature logs (must remain 35-75°F)
4. Count all units/pallets and compare against packing list
5. Note any discrepancies on the receiving report

## 4. Inspection

### 4.1 Standard Inspection
- Visual inspection of all units for packaging integrity
- Verify product labeling (ingredients, warnings, expiration dates)
- Certificate of conformance verification
- Match lot/batch numbers to documentation
- Check expiration/best-by dates (minimum 6 months remaining for food products)

### 4.2 Enhanced Inspection (Required for)
- New pet product supplier first shipment: **100% inspection**
- Supplier under quality escalation: **100% inspection**
- Alternate supplier initial orders: **100% inspection for first 3 shipments**
- Pet food and health products: **Temperature log verification + 25% sample inspection**
- Pet toys and accessories (choking hazard check): **15% sample inspection**

### 4.3 Pet Product Safety Checks
- Verify AAFCO compliance labels on all pet food
- Check for recall notices against current FDA/CPSC recall list
- Inspect toy products for small parts, sharp edges, toxic materials
- Verify flea/tick medications have intact tamper-evident seals

## 5. Inventory Update

1. Update inventory system within **4 hours** of receipt completion
2. Record actual received quantities and lot numbers
3. Update days-of-supply calculation for retail partner allocation
4. Flag any items that bring inventory above max capacity
5. Update product freshness rotation (FIFO for all food products)

## 6. Discrepancy Handling

### 6.1 Quantity Discrepancy
- Shortages > 5%: Notify procurement within 2 hours; file carrier claim
- Overages: Receive and notify procurement for PO adjustment

### 6.2 Quality/Safety Rejection
- Segregate rejected material in quarantine area
- Notify supplier and procurement within 4 hours
- Generate non-conformance report (NCR)
- For pet food safety concerns: notify Quality team immediately for potential recall assessment
- If rejection causes stockout risk: trigger expedited shipping per PET-POL-004

## 7. Late Arrival Processing

When a shipment arrives past the expected delivery date:
1. Record actual arrival date and calculate delay days
2. If delay > 5 days: create incident record
3. If delay > 10 days: notify Pet Supply Chain Manager for penalty assessment per contract terms
4. Update supplier performance metrics automatically
5. Assess impact on retail partner fulfillment commitments
