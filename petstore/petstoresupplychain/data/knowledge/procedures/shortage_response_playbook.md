# Pet Product Shortage Response Playbook

**Document ID:** PET-PLAY-003
**Effective Date:** February 15, 2024
**Owner:** VP Pet Supply Chain Operations
**Classification:** Internal Operations – Critical Response

---

## 1. Purpose

This playbook provides a structured decision framework for responding to pet product shortages and stockout risks across the retail supply chain, ensuring minimal disruption to retail partners and pet owners.

## 2. Shortage Severity Classification

| Level | Days of Supply | Response Time | Decision Authority |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Watch** | 5–10 days | 48 hours | Pet Supply Chain Analyst |
| **Warning** | 3–5 days | 24 hours | Pet Supply Chain Manager |
| **Critical** | < 3 days | Immediate | Director, Pet Supply Chain Ops |
| **Stockout** | 0 days | Immediate | VP Pet Supply Chain |

## 3. Response Procedures

### 3.1 Watch Level (5–10 Days of Supply)

1. Verify open purchase orders and expected delivery dates
2. Contact pet product supplier to confirm shipment status
3. Review retail partner demand forecast for next 14 days
4. Prepare expedited shipping request if standard replenishment insufficient
5. Update status daily
6. Check if affected products are on upcoming promotional calendars

### 3.2 Warning Level (3–5 Days of Supply)

1. All Watch-level actions plus:
2. Notify warehouse manager and retail fulfillment planning
3. Submit expedited shipping request per PET-POL-004 (Manager approval)
4. Contact alternate pet suppliers on AVL for availability check
5. Assess retail partner order impact and notify Sales if commitments at risk
6. Update status twice daily
7. Consider cross-warehouse transfer if other locations have surplus

### 3.3 Critical Level (< 3 Days of Supply)

1. All Warning-level actions plus:
2. **Escalate immediately to Director, Pet Supply Chain Operations**
3. Authorize expedited/air freight shipping (Director approval per PET-POL-004 Section 2.3)
4. Activate emergency qualification of alternate supplier if on AVL (72-hour process)
5. Implement retail partner allocation rationing if multiple partners affected
6. Initiate supplier escalation per Tier 2/3 of PET-RUN-001 if delay is supplier-caused
7. Hourly status updates to stakeholders
8. For pet food products: verify remaining stock freshness dates

### 3.4 Stockout (0 Days of Supply)

1. All Critical-level actions plus:
2. **Immediate VP Pet Supply Chain notification**
3. Retail partner impact assessment within 2 hours
4. Customer communication plan (through retail partners) within 4 hours
5. Emergency procurement authorization (VP approval, any source)
6. Post-incident review scheduled within 5 business days
7. For essential products (pet food, medications): priority emergency sourcing

## 4. Cross-Functional Notification Matrix

|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|

| Stockout | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ | VP + SVP |

## 5. Recovery and Post-Incident

1. Document root cause and contributing factors
2. Update supplier performance record
3. Assess financial impact (expedited costs, retail partner penalties, lost revenue)
4. Revise safety stock levels if systemic issue identified
5. Update this playbook if gaps identified
6. Share lessons learned with retail partner account managers
